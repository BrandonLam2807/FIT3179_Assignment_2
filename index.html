Natural Disaster across the map of Australia

This map shows how different natural disasters affect Australia. Along the northern coast, cyclones and floods are common, while in the south and east, bushfires are more frequent. The larger the circle, the more people were affected by the disaster. In the southeast, where most people live, many types of disasters overlap, making this area especially vulnerable. The map highlights how various regions of Australia face different threats, showing the need for communities to be prepared for these recurring challenges.
